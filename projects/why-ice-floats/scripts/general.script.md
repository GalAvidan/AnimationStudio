# Why Does Ice Float? — General Script

**Duration:** ~30 seconds  
**Audience:** General  
**Variant:** general

---

## Beat 1 — Hook (0–5s)

[VISUAL: A glass of water. An ice cube drops in and floats to the surface.]

Take a glass of water. Drop in an ice cube.

It floats.

---

## Beat 2 — Contrast (5–10s)

[VISUAL: A rock and a coin fall into the same glass and sink to the bottom.]
[SPEAKER: curious]

A rock. A coin. They sink.

Every solid we know sinks in liquid.

---

## Beat 3 — The Strange Thing (10–15s)

[VISUAL: An ice cube is placed in the water. Unlike the rock and coin, it rises back to the surface.]
[SPEAKER: curious]

So why does *solid* water float on *liquid* water?

---

## Beat 4 — Molecules (15–22s)

[VISUAL: Seven scattered molecules (liquid state). At the lattice-form sync point they snap into a hexagonal arrangement and bond lines appear.]

[SYNC: lattice-form]

When water freezes, its molecules lock into a hexagonal cage —

— spreading them farther apart.

---

## Beat 5 — Density (22–27s)

[VISUAL: Side-by-side blocks — water (left) and ice (right). At the density-reveal sync point, the ice block expands and floats upward.]

[SYNC: density-reveal]

Same molecules. More space. Less dense.

It floats.

---

## Beat 6 — Payoff (27–30s)

[VISUAL: A lake cross-section — ice on top, cold water below, deep water with fish.]
[SPEAKER: curious]

That's why lakes freeze from the top down —

keeping everything underneath alive.

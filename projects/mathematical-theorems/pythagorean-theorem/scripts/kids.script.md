# Script: Pythagorean Theorem — Kids Audience

## Working Title

A fun, bouncy proof that A² + B² = C² on a right triangle, using a 3-4-5 example.

## Audience

Curious kids (roughly 8–12) who know what a square and a triangle are but haven't met the Pythagorean theorem yet. They leave knowing that squaring the two short sides of a right triangle and adding them gives you the square of the long side.

## Target Runtime

~60 seconds

## Tone

Playful, warm, celebratory. Bouncy springs, bright colors on cream. Numbers feel like little characters that pop and high-five.

## Pacing

~2–2.5 spoken words per second. Each beat lists a target word count. Leave a short pause (~0.5 s) near beat boundaries to let visuals breathe before and after speaking.

**Time:** 0–6 s | **Duration:** 6 s | **Target words:** ~14
**Caption on screen:** *"90°"* — fades in near the right-angle marker after it pops

Meet a very special triangle — one with a perfectly square corner. That little square means the angle is exactly ninety degrees.

[VISUAL: Triangle bounces in from below and settles. A small filled square pops on at the right-angle corner. Caption "90°" fades in beside the marker, then fades out.]
[SYNC: triangle-in]

### Beat: labels — Label Sides 3, 4, and C
**Time:** 6–13 s | **Duration:** 7 s | **Target words:** ~17
**Caption on screen:** *(no new caption — equation "A² + B² = ?" types in on screen)*

The two short sides measure three and four. The long side, going corner to corner, is a mystery — let's call it C. Can we figure out what C is?

[VISUAL: Numbers 3, 4, and "C" fly in and stick to their sides. The equation "A² + B² = ?" types in above the triangle simultaneously.]
[SYNC: legs-labeled]

---

### Beat: square-a — Square A = 9
**Time:** 13–22 s | **Duration:** 9 s | **Target words:** ~22
**Caption on screen:** *"3 × 3 = 9"* — fades in above the red square once all dots are counted

Here's the trick. Take the side that's three and build a square on it. Three rows, three columns — count them up: nine little squares. Three squared equals nine.

[VISUAL: A red 3×3 square unfolds outward off the short leg. A grid of 9 dots fills in one by one; a counter ticks 1 → 9. Caption "3 × 3 = 9" fades in when counting completes, then fades out.]
[SYNC: square-a-pop]

---

### Beat: square-b — Square B = 16
**Time:** 22–31 s | **Duration:** 9 s | **Target words:** ~22
**Caption on screen:** *"4 × 4 = 16"* — fades in to the right of the blue square once all dots are counted

Do the same for the side that's four. Four rows, four columns — sixteen little squares. Four squared equals sixteen.

[VISUAL: A blue 4×4 square unfolds outward off the long leg. A grid of 16 dots fills in; counter ticks 1 → 16. Caption "4 × 4 = 16" fades in when counting completes, then fades out.]
[SYNC: square-b-pop]

---

### Beat: equation — Values Dock Under the Equation
**Time:** 31–39 s | **Duration:** 8 s | **Target words:** ~19
**Caption on screen:** *(no new caption — squared tokens animate to dock under A² and B²)*

Nine over here, sixteen over there. What happens if we slide them together and add them up?

[VISUAL: Tokens "3²" and "4²" fly from their squares to the equation row and cross-fade into "9" and "16" as they dock under "A²" and "B²".]
[SYNC: equation-appear]

---

### Beat: sum — 9 + 16 = 25, Green Square Unfolds
**Time:** 39–51 s | **Duration:** 12 s | **Target words:** ~28
**Caption on screen:** *"5 × 5 = 25"* — fades in to the right of the green square after "25" lands on it

Nine plus sixteen is… twenty-five! Now look at the long side — build a square on it too. Five rows, five columns: twenty-five squares. The same number!

[VISUAL: "+" and "=" pop between 9 and 16; "25" bursts in with sparkles. A green 5×5 square unfolds along the hypotenuse; 25 dots fill it; the "25" token flies from the equation and lands on the square with a bounce. Caption "5 × 5 = 25" fades in.]
[SYNC: sum-collide]
[SYNC: hypotenuse-square]

---

### Beat: verify — A² + B² = C² ✓
**Time:** 51–60 s | **Duration:** 9 s | **Target words:** ~28
**Caption on screen:** *"Works for every right triangle!"* — fades in at the lower-right as the theorem is confirmed

So C squared equals twenty-five — which means C itself equals five. A squared plus B squared equals C squared. Every single time, for every right triangle. That's the Pythagorean theorem!

[VISUAL: "C²" replaces "?" in the equation. Derivation "C² = 25 → C = 5" appears and cross-fades. Hypotenuse label morphs "C" → "5" with sparkles. A ✓ pops on. Triangle breathes gently. Caption "Works for every right triangle!" fades in and stays.]
[SYNC: verify-equals]

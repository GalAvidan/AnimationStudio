# Script: Pythagorean Theorem — Adults Audience

## Working Title

A tight, playful refresher of A² + B² = C² on a right triangle, proven visually with a 3-4-5 example.

## Audience

Adults who learned the Pythagorean theorem once and forgot why it works. They leave with the geometric intuition: the squares of the two legs literally add up — by area — to the square of the hypotenuse.

## Target Runtime

~55 seconds

## Tone

Playful but a little drier than the kids cut. Same bright palette, slightly tighter pacing, fewer sparkles, math vocabulary used confidently ("legs", "hypotenuse", "area").

---

## Script

A right triangle — two legs and a hypotenuse, joined at a perfect ninety-degree corner.

[VISUAL: Triangle slides in, bounces once, settles. Filled square appears in the right-angle corner.]
[SYNC: triangle-in]

---

Legs of length **3** and **4**. Hypotenuse of length **5**.

[VISUAL: Numbers 3, 4, 5 fly onto their sides.]
[SYNC: legs-labeled]

---

Build a square on the first leg. Side length three, area **nine**.

[VISUAL: Red 3×3 square unfolds outward. Dot grid fills it; counter ticks to 9.]
[SYNC: square-a-pop]

---

Same move on the second leg. Side length four, area **sixteen**.

[VISUAL: Blue 4×4 square unfolds outward. Dot grid fills it; counter ticks to 16.]
[SYNC: square-b-pop]

---

Two areas on the legs. Add them.

[VISUAL: Equation "A² + B² = ?" types in. The 9 and 16 dock beneath their terms.]
[SYNC: equation-appear]

---

**Twenty-five.** Which is exactly the area of the square on the hypotenuse.

[VISUAL: 9 and 16 collide into 25 with a small poof. Green 5×5 square unfolds off the hypotenuse and the 25 lands on it.]
[SYNC: sum-collide]
[SYNC: hypotenuse-square]

---

That's the theorem: **A² + B² = C²**. Not algebra — geometry. The areas literally add.

[VISUAL: Equation completes to "9 + 16 = 25 ✓". "A² + B² = C²" settles below the triangle. Gentle breathe.]
[SYNC: verify-equals]

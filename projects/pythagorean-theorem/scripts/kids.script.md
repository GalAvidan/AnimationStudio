# Script: Pythagorean Theorem — Kids Audience

## Working Title

A fun, bouncy proof that A² + B² = C² on a right triangle, using a 3-4-5 example.

## Audience

Curious kids (roughly 8–12) who know what a square and a triangle are but haven't met the Pythagorean theorem yet. They leave knowing that squaring the two short sides of a right triangle and adding them gives you the square of the long side.

## Target Runtime

~60 seconds

## Tone

Playful, warm, celebratory. Bouncy springs, bright colors on cream. Numbers feel like little characters that pop and high-five.

---

## Script

Meet a triangle. But not just any triangle — this one has a perfectly square corner.

[VISUAL: Triangle slides in from below, bounces once, settles. A small filled square appears in the right-angle corner.]
[SYNC: triangle-in]

---

Its two short sides are **3** and **4**. The long side across from the square corner? That's **5**.

[VISUAL: Numbers 3, 4, 5 fly in and stick to each side with a tick sound.]
[SYNC: legs-labeled]

---

Now watch this trick. Take the side that's **3** and build a square on it. Three across, three down — that's **9 little squares**.

[VISUAL: A red 3×3 square unfolds outward off the short leg. A grid of 9 dots fills it; a counter ticks 1, 2, 3 … 9.]
[SYNC: square-a-pop]

---

Do the same with the side that's **4**. Four across, four down — **16 little squares**.

[VISUAL: A blue 4×4 square unfolds outward off the long leg. A grid of 16 dots fills it; a counter ticks up to 16.]
[SYNC: square-b-pop]

---

So we have **9** over here, and **16** over there. What if we add them up?

[VISUAL: The equation "A² + B² = ?" types in above the triangle. The 9 and 16 shrink and dock under "A²" and "B²".]
[SYNC: equation-appear]

---

Nine plus sixteen… **twenty-five!** And look — that's exactly the square on the longest side.

[VISUAL: The 9 and 16 slide together, collide with a sparkle poof, merge into 25. A green 5×5 square unfolds off the hypotenuse and the 25 lands on top of it.]
[SYNC: sum-collide]
[SYNC: hypotenuse-square]

---

**A² plus B² equals C²** — every single time. That's the Pythagorean theorem. Pretty cool, right?

[VISUAL: Equation completes to "9 + 16 = 25 ✓". A large "A² + B² = C²" title settles below the triangle. Triangle breathes gently. Sparkles.]
[SYNC: verify-equals]
